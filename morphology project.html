Introduction

The morphology of flowering plants involves the study of the form and structure of their flowers. This includes understanding various flower parts and their functions.

Flower Parts

Flowers consist of several parts, each with specific roles:

  • Petals: Colorful parts that attract pollinators.
  • Sepals: Protective outer layer of the flower bud.
  • Stamens: Male reproductive parts that produce pollen.
  • Carpels: Female reproductive parts that house the ovules.

Types of Flowers

Flowers can be categorized based on various characteristics:

  • Complete Flowers: Have all four major parts (sepals, petals, stamens, and carpels).
  • Incomplete Flowers: Lack one or more of the major parts.
  • Perfect Flowers: Contain both stamens and carpels.
  • Imperfect Flowers: Either have stamens or carpels, but not both.
